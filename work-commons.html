Commons Clinic

I conducted competitor UX flow analysis, led light UX research using AI, translated insights into actionable design decisions, and presented findings, concepts, and future brand direction to stakeholders.

Year 2025
Industry Healthcare
Role Bella Wood

The Challenge

How do you transform a website into a product experience?

Before designing anything, I needed clarity. A strong product experience starts with understanding business goals, user behavior, and the gap between a traditional marketing site and a functional digital product.

Commons Clinic asked me to reimagine their website as a unified, in-platform experience. Patients should be able to explore care options, learn about providers, and book appointments—without leaving the ecosystem. The experience needed to feel modern, premium, and trustworthy while aligning with the core brand.

Step 1: Context & Product Definition

A website markets.
A product serves.

Website: Primarily focused on branding, messaging, and converting traffic through static content.

Product experience: Interactive, functional, and built around task completion. It supports ongoing engagement, clear user goals, and continuous iteration.

The opportunity was to shift Commons Clinic from the first to the second.

Step 2: User Research & Flow Analysis

User Flow Mapping

I analyzed the current Commons Clinic site alongside competitor and adjacent product experiences such as Hims and Airbnb.

The goal was to understand how successful platforms guide users smoothly toward a clear outcome.

Since I did not have access to live users, I conducted structured research using my self-built UX Research AI Agent. Through RTCCF-based prompting and iterative dialogue, I simulated interview insights and friction points to surface patterns in navigation, clarity, and decision-making.

Insights Summary

  • Bring booking fully in-platform
  • Provide clear confirmation and next steps
  • Make primary user paths immediately visible on the homepage
  • Use interactive elements to signal functionality
  • Introduce user profiles and login for continuity

Competitor / Similar Product UX Inspection

Layout Review

I evaluated what works—and what does not—across similar digital health products.

The key question: Where are users guided? and what patterns emerge across these products?

By identifying effective patterns, I curated a cleaner and more intentional structure for Commons Clinic. Some patterns and features I found:

  • Main product action area in the hero section
  • Three or more categories of action for users, different from the main action
  • User profile / simplified bar in the top right
  • Trust-building banner on top
  • Hint to scroll further with content half in view

Turning Insights into Concepts

Concept Development

With research complete, I distilled findings into tangible product decisions.

Navigation became task-driven.
Booking became seamless.
Interaction became purposeful.

The experience began to feel less like a website—and more like a platform.

Step 3: Design & Prototyping

Product Experience Design

This is my favorite phase.

While designing the new layout, I prioritized:

  • Clear product navigation
  • Strong visual hierarchy
  • Functional interaction and animations

The homepage presents three primary user routes immediately above the fold, making it easy for patients to choose their path.

I introduced a User Profile system to lay the groundwork for a future app experience. Within the profile, patients can manage appointments, explore providers, access personalized care plans, and store documents. This shift anchors the site as a true digital product.

Hover interactions and structured storytelling guide users down the page while maintaining a premium healthcare tone.

Step 4: Stakeholder Presentation & Brand Direction

Presentation & Feedback

I presented the work to Commons Clinic stakeholders including the CEO, Marketing Director, and Creative Team Lead.

They responded positively to the user and competitive analysis. The UI design received strong praise, though they felt the creative direction pushed the brand further than expected. That tension is common in healthcare—it is often the designer’s role to show what is possible, then calibrate if needed.

Conclusion

This work repositioned Commons Clinic from a static informational site into the foundation of a scalable product ecosystem.

By combining UX research, product thinking, and thoughtful visual design, I shaped a direction that simplifies the healthcare journey while strengthening trust and clarity.

The result is not just a redesign. It is a vision for a cohesive, patient-centered digital experience.
